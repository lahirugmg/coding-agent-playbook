# Skill: process-analysis

**Type:** atomic

## Purpose

Map an existing business process, identify gaps, inefficiencies, and failure points, and propose an improved future-state process. Used before designing a system change that affects how people or systems do work.

## When to Invoke

- A workflow is being automated, redesigned, or replaced and the current state must be understood first.
- Stakeholders report that a process is broken or slow, but the specific pain points are unclear.
- A system change will affect multiple teams and the impact needs to be mapped.

## Workflow

1. **Define the scope.** Establish the process boundary: where does the process start and where does it end? Name the roles involved and the systems they use.

2. **Map the current state (As-Is).** Walk through the process step by step with the people who actually do it — not just their managers. For each step, capture:
   - Who performs it (role, not name)
   - What system or tool is used
   - Inputs and outputs
   - Approximate time taken
   - Known failure modes or workarounds

3. **Identify pain points.** Annotate the As-Is map with:
   - **Bottlenecks** — steps that slow the process or create backlogs
   - **Handoff failures** — points where information is lost between roles or systems
   - **Manual workarounds** — steps that exist only because a system can't do something it should
   - **Redundancy** — the same work done more than once

4. **Quantify impact.** For the most significant pain points, estimate the cost: time lost per cycle, error rate, frequency of re-work. This data justifies prioritisation.

5. **Design the future state (To-Be).** Propose an improved process that addresses the identified pain points. For each change, state:
   - What changes and why
   - What the change enables or removes
   - Any new dependencies or risks introduced

6. **Identify gaps.** List capabilities required by the To-Be process that do not currently exist — in systems, in skills, or in team structure.

7. **Validate.** Walk through the To-Be process with the stakeholders who will operate it. Confirm it is feasible and that the pain points have been addressed.

## Output

- **As-Is process map** — current state with annotated pain points and bottlenecks
- **Pain point register** — each issue with estimated impact
- **To-Be process map** — proposed future state with rationale for each change
- **Gap list** — capabilities required that do not currently exist
- **Validation record** — confirmation from process owners that the To-Be is feasible
