# Skill: roadmap

**Type:** atomic

## Purpose

Build or update a prioritised product roadmap that communicates what will be built, in what order, and why — without over-committing to specific dates. A roadmap is a strategic communication tool, not a project plan.

## When to Invoke

- A product or feature area needs a multi-quarter plan for stakeholder communication.
- An existing roadmap needs to be updated to reflect changed priorities or new information.
- Stakeholders are asking "what's next?" and there is no structured answer.

## Workflow

1. **Gather input.** Collect: business goals and OKRs for the period, validated requirements and PRDs, known dependencies, team capacity signals, and any committed deadlines. Do not build a roadmap without knowing the business goals it is meant to serve.

2. **Define the time horizon.** Roadmaps work at three horizons:
   - **Now** (current quarter): committed, detailed, specific
   - **Next** (next 1–2 quarters): directional, may change, some detail
   - **Later** (beyond): themes only, expected to change significantly
   Avoid putting specific features in the Later horizon — themes are more honest.

3. **Identify themes.** Group related initiatives into themes (e.g. "Reliability", "Enterprise readiness", "User onboarding"). Themes communicate strategy; features communicate tactics.

4. **Prioritise.** For each item in the Now and Next horizons, apply a consistent prioritisation model. A simple model: score each item on Value (impact to users/business), Confidence (how well-understood it is), and Effort (cost to deliver). Rank by Value/Effort ratio, weighted by Confidence.

5. **Map dependencies.** Identify items that must be completed before others can start. Make these visible on the roadmap — hidden dependencies are the most common cause of roadmap failure.

6. **Document assumptions.** Each roadmap item rests on assumptions (about demand, feasibility, team capacity). State them explicitly so that when assumptions change, the roadmap update is obvious.

7. **Communicate constraints.** Any item with a hard external deadline (regulatory, contractual, seasonal) must be flagged as a constraint, not a priority — it is not negotiable.

8. **Review and update cadence.** A roadmap without a review cadence becomes stale. Define when it will be reviewed (typically quarterly for strategic items, monthly for tactical).

## Output

- **Roadmap document** — Now/Next/Later view with themes and items
- **Prioritisation rationale** — how items were ranked and why
- **Dependency map** — items that block or are blocked by others
- **Assumptions log** — stated assumptions for each significant item
- **Constraint register** — hard deadlines and non-negotiable commitments
